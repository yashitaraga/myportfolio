By: Yashita Raga Saranam and Group 9 Members

Overview

This project investigates the various genetic, lifestyle, and environmental factors contributing to baldness. Using a dataset of 999 individuals from Kaggle, we analyzed 12 variables to identify key risk contributors to hair loss using R for data cleaning, visualization, and statistical modeling.

Objectives

  • Determine whether factors like stress, hormones, smoking, and nutrition have a significant impact on baldness.
  • Apply Chi-square testing and multiple logistic regression models for statistical analysis.
  • Use feature selection and interaction terms to refine model accuracy.

Key Findings

  • Initial tests found no significant individual predictors; however, after feature selection, medical conditions such as thyroid problems showed strong association with baldness.
  • Smoking, especially when combined with eczema, had a significant interaction effect contributing to hair loss.
  • The final model rejected the null hypothesis, confirming that at least one factor among the evaluated variables does influence baldness.

Technologies Used

  • R (Data Cleaning, Logistic Regression, Visualization)
  • Chi-square Tests, One-hot Encoding, Q-Q Plots, Log Transformations
